Cloudflare

Introduction

Cloudflare is a global web infrastructure company that helps improve the security, performance, and reliability of websites and online services. It acts as an intermediary between users and web servers, filtering traffic and optimizing delivery.

What Does Cloudflare Do?

Cloudflare provides services such as content delivery networks (CDN), DDoS protection, web application firewalls, and DNS management. These services help protect websites from attacks while making them load faster for users around the world.

Why Cloudflare Is Important

By handling large amounts of internet traffic and blocking malicious requests, Cloudflare plays a key role in modern web security. Many organizations rely on Cloudflare to keep their websites available, fast, and secure.
